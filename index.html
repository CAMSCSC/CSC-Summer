CAMS Computer Science Enrichment

A computer science and cyber security summer enrichment program for middle school students.
General Information

The CAMS Computer Science Enrichment (CCSE) is a summer enrichment program run by the computer science team at the California Academy of Mathematics and Science (CAMS). The program aims to teach middle school students interested in the fields of Electrical Engineering, Cyber Security, or Computer Science about advanced and fascinating topics that are usually never covered in computer science classes. Topics in this session include: Programming, Forensics, Exploitation (Web & Binary), and Cryptography.

After comprehensive lessons, we will allow the students to demonstrate their skills in challenges that we have set up. On the last day of the camp, students will compete in a full length Capture the Flag (CTF) hacking competition using the skills they've learned with prizes awarded to top placing teams.

Target Audience

CCSE is made for current middle school students and rising freshmen (those entering 9th grade after summer). However, younger or older participants can also join as this program is designed to accommodate students of all skill levels.

Topics Covered

Cryptography

Learn about the basic principles of encryption and decryption in computer science. Discover common forms of cryptographic ciphers and frequently used symmetric, asymmetric, and one-way schemes including hashes, XOR encryption, SSL, and RSA.

Exploitation

Break into vulnerable web applications and reverse engineer simple binary applications. Learn about basic exploitation frameworks suck as Kali Linux, tactics such vulnerability assessment, and ethics such as white-hat hacking. Engage in realistic hacking operations on a vulnerable server.

Forensics

Find the needle in a haystack of information on a hard drive. Learn about concepts such as steganography, virtual machines, file signatures, and deleted file recovery. Analyze partitions of a flash drive and uncover secrets hidden within files.

Programming

Write code to solve complex tasks that are too tedious to accomplish manually. Plan simple algorithms to accomplish a search task or extraction task in the shortest time possible. Design offensive software to automatically exploit vulnerabilities on a server.

Our Qualifications

The CAMS Computer Science and Cyber Security Club (CSC) is a relatively new club at the school. However, the team has already had many impressive accomplishments. In 2013, our team placed first in the 10th annual national High School Forensics competition at NYU Polytechnic Institute. In 2014, we returned as a national level qualifying team. This year, we ran CAMS CTF, an international computer science and hacking competition that attracted over 2000 students from around the world.

We have also written curriculum using GitBook titled "CSC Learn" that teaches simple to intermediate level computer science topics.

FAQ

Fear not. We are here to answer your frequently asked questions.

  • What is CTF?

    CTF or Capture the Flag is different in the computer science world. In this area, CTF is a competition in which teams solve challenges and find 'flags.' These flags are not physical flags. Instead, they are strings of characters that can be submitted for points.

  • Where does the money go?

    Most of the money goes to covering server costs, infrastructure costs, supervisor compensation, insurance, and most importantly - food. All additional earnings are saved for future club spending. We do not make any money for personal profit.

  • I know nothing about computers!

    Perfect! We would love to teach you. As long as you have a passion or interest in this area, you will most likely enjoy this summer session a lot. We started out just like you.

  • What is the purpose of this program?

    As a club, we are really passionate about computer science and cyber security. We feel that classes like Computer Science AP do not focus on the fun and exciting parts of industry. Thus, we founded CSC and made it our goal to teach skills that are vital in industry but often missed in classes. In addition, we want students to get a head start so they can be more prepared to enter the diverse field of science and technology.

  • But hacking is illegal?

    Some types of hacking are illegal. These types of illicit hackers are called "black-hat" hackers. They use their skills for malicious purpose or personal gain. We are "white-hat" hackers. We hack purely for educational purposes and cyber security. Offense sometimes makes the best defense.

  • That's a lot of stuff to cover.

    Yup. We have a lot of stuff planned. But we will get through all of it in a fun and understandable manner. We're not college professors lecturing with obscure jargon.

  • What if I'm not a middle school student?

    Contact us via email and we will be happy to talk about special circumstances.

  • I have more questions.

    Email us at camscsc@gmail.com!
